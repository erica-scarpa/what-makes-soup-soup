What Makes Soup, Soup?

In a hilarious BBC Scotland sketch, a character embarks on an existential quest to answer the age-old question: "What makes soup, soup?" As he spirals through debates about sauce, gravy, gazpacho, and porridge, his confusion only deepens. It’s funny, relatable—and surprisingly relevant to Heritage Science. Not only because food preparation is a form of Heritage, but also because Heritage itself is notoriously tricky to define. The more we try to pin it down, the more elusive it becomes. One thing we can agree on is that the concept of Heritage is constantly evolving. What we consider "Heritage" today is the result of centuries of transformation. Change, it seems, is the only constant—and this dynamic nature is precisely why defining the domain is such a challenging task. The Heritage Science Ontology Working Group is dedicated to fostering discussion and collaboration around defining both Heritage and the broader Heritage Science domain. By engaging with these complexities, we aim to bring clarity to a field that thrives on its fluid and ever-changing nature.
